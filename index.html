About Myanmar Instruments

Myanmar has a rich musical tradition with instruments that reflect the country’s diverse cultures and history. The most iconic is the saung gauk, a beautifully curved harp considered the national instrument. Traditional ensembles often feature the pat waing, a circular drum set with multiple tuned drums, and the kyi waing and maung hsaing, which are bronze gong circles that create melodic patterns. The hne, a powerful double-reed wind instrument, leads many performances with its bright, piercing tone. You’ll also find instruments like the si and wa (clapper and bell), the tapa drums, and various bamboo flutes, all contributing to the distinctive sound of Myanmar’s classical, folk, and ceremonial music.
